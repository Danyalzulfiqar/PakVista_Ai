Sikhism

Gurus

Sikh faith

The Origins of Sikhism in Pakistan
The
Sikh religion
emerged as a beacon of truth, equality, and spirituality during a time of religious and social upheaval. Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism, was born in
Nankana Sahib
, located in present-day
Punjab, Pakistan
. His teachings emphasized devotion to one God, selfless service, and the rejection of caste-based discrimination.
Pakistan, particularly the Punjab region, is a sacred land where several significant events in the lives of Sikh Gurus took place. From Guru Nanak’s birthplace to the establishment of the first Sikh communities, Pakistan holds a pivotal place in Sikh history.
Top Sikh Holy Sites in Pakistan
1. Gurdwara Janam Asthan, Nankana Sahib
Significance:
Birthplace of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism.
Location:
Nankana Sahib, Punjab, Pakistan.
Importance:
This gurdwara is the most sacred site for Sikhs globally, attracting thousands of devotees every year for religious pilgrimage. It is part of the
Sikh Yatra
or spiritual journey for Sikhs.
2. Gurdwara Darbar Sahib, Kartarpur (Kartarpur Sahib)
Significance:
Final resting place of Guru Nanak Dev Ji.
Location:
Kartarpur, near the Indian border.
Importance:
Known as the
Kartarpur Corridor
, this site allows Sikh pilgrims from India and around the world to visit without requiring a visa. It symbolizes peace, devotion, and Guru Nanak’s message of unity.
3. Gurdwara Panja Sahib, Hassan Abdal
Significance:
Associated with Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s handprint on a rock.
Location:
Hassan Abdal, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan.
Importance:
This site is a key pilgrimage destination during
Baisakhi celebrations
and holds great spiritual value in Sikhism.
4. Gurdwara Sacha Sauda, Farooqabad
Significance:
Site where Guru Nanak performed an act of selfless charity.
Location:
Sheikhupura, Punjab.
Importance:
This gurdwara represents the core Sikh belief of
Seva
(selfless service) and charity.
5. Gurdwara Rori Sahib, Eminabad
Significance:
Guru Nanak Dev Ji visited this site during Babar’s invasion.
Location:
Eminabad, near Gujranwala.
Importance:
A place of reflection and resilience, marking an important historical event in Sikh history.
6. Samadhi of Maharaja Ranjit Singh, Lahore
Significance:
Memorial of the great Sikh ruler Maharaja Ranjit Singh.
Location:
Adjacent to Gurdwara Dera Sahib, Lahore.
Importance:
Maharaja Ranjit Singh united the Sikh Empire and played a key role in preserving Sikh heritage.
