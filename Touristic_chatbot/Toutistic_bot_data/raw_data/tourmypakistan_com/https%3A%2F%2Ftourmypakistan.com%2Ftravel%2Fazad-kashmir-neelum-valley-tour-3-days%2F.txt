Places You’ll See :
Arang Kel, situated in the Azad Jammu and Kashmir region, is a hidden treasure waiting to be discovered. This picturesque village is perched on a ridge that overlooks the Neelum Valley, offering visitors breathtaking panoramic views of lush forests, towering mountains, and the pristine Neelum River meandering below. Although reaching Arang Kel requires a moderately challenging trek, it's the perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and hikers seeking solitude and natural beauty. The village is dotted with charming wooden huts, terraced fields, and inhabited by the friendly Gujjar community. Arang Kel provides a tranquil escape from the noise and chaos of city life, allowing you to immerse yourself in the serene and untouched landscapes of Pakistan-administered Kashmir.

Arang Kel
Gulmit, a picturesque village nestled in the heart of the Hunza Valley, Pakistan, is a tranquil escape into the lap of the Karakoram Mountains. This idyllic village exudes a sense of serenity and timelessness, with its terraced fields, traditional stone houses, and friendly local community. Gulmit offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and culture, making it a hub for trekkers and nature enthusiasts. It's a gateway to some of the most stunning treks and adventures in the region, including the nearby Passu Glacier and Borith Lake. Visitors to Gulmit are treated not only to breathtaking landscapes but also to the warm hospitality of the people who call this charming village home. It's a place where time seems to stand still, and the beauty of nature takes center stage.

Nestled against the backdrop of the Margalla Hills, Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan, stands as a striking embodiment of modernity and natural beauty. This dynamic metropolis is a harmonious blend of urban sophistication and natural tranquility, offering an array of experiences for residents and visitors alike.
<strong>Natural Splendor:</strong> The city's proximity to the Margalla Hills National Park invites outdoor enthusiasts to explore the lush hiking trails, while the serene Rawal Lake provides a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of urban life.
<strong>Architectural Grandeur:</strong> Islamabad is graced with striking architecture and modern landmarks, including the awe-inspiring Faisal Mosque, one of the world's largest mosques, and Daman-e-Koh, a viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the city.
<strong>Cultural Vibrancy:</strong> The city's diverse cultural scene thrives with galleries, museums, and bustling markets. You can explore Lok Virsa Museum to delve into Pakistan's rich heritage or indulge in shopping at F-6 Supermarket.
<strong>Culinary Delights:</strong> Islamabad's culinary landscape is a delightful fusion of local and international flavors. The city's vibrant food scene offers everything from traditional Pakistani cuisine to global culinary experiences.
<strong>Urban Sophistication:</strong> As the political and administrative hub of Pakistan, Islamabad boasts well-planned neighborhoods, modern infrastructure, and an efficient transport system, making it a comfortable and well-connected city.
Whether you're looking to explore the city's cultural heritage, embark on an adventure in the Margalla Hills, or simply relax by the lake, Islamabad welcomes all with open arms. It's a city where modernity and nature coexist in perfect harmony, offering an enchanting experience for every traveler.
